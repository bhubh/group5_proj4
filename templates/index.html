Future of US Renewable Energy

Our team analyzed the current production of clean energy and the distance away from our realistic reliance on clean energy as the U.S. main energy source. We realize that there is a need for cheap clean energy sources, but we also realize that it could be costly to transition away traditional sources of energy like fossil fuels and natural gas. We attempted to design a machine learning model that uses linear regression to predict the amount of clean energy production and consumption over the next two decades. In conclusion, we realized that with increasing population, energy prices, and energy consumption that the United States has a long way to go to meet our energy consumption needs with only renewable energy. Based on our current predictions it would take a drastic change in current energy practices and production to meet these increasing demands. We are encouraged by the recent “Inflation Reduction Act” that will put $370 billion towards combating climate change and increasing US clean energy production, while reducing reliance on traditional sources of fossil fuels.
